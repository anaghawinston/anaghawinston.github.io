Who are Anagha & Winston?

We're juniors who want to change how students interact with Student Government

Favorite Way to Unwind: Watercoloring

Anagha Kikkeri: President
Anagha has been a part of UT’s student government and has been involved in the foundational efforts of diversity and inclusion for UPC. Since becoming involved in an institution that has structural issues inherently detracting from diversity, she has been inspired to implement change in places that people deem as “unchangeable.” Gathering from her experiences tackling diversity and inclusion in Greek life, Anagha wants to bring this reform to a university-wide platform. She has seen how student government operates from the inside and wants to expand its scope so more students can benefit from the services that it offers. It is Anagha’s firm belief that no matter what the student’s background is, every student deserves their voice to be heard and represented on this campus.

Winston Hung: Vice President
Winston is a 1st generation American from a small town in Texas called Beaumont and didn’t have many resources, mentors, and networks coming into UT. Since coming onto campus, Winston has explored several different groups and never thought that he was "well-connected" enough to join something like Student Government. Winston landed where he is now and built his own network of mentors to pay it forward by getting involved with several mentorship programs. Now, Winston wants to pay it forward even further on an institutional level by specifically impacting students like him who may not have advantages or resources coming into college, who consequently aren’t as comfortable in a civic space like SG.
